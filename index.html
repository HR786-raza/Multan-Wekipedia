Overview

Multan is a historic city located in the Punjab province of Pakistan. It is known as the "City of Saints" because of the large number of
shrines and spiritual leaders buried there.[1]

History

Multan is one of the oldest cities in South Asia, with a history dating back more than 2,000 years. It was an important center of trade and culture during the Islamic and pre-Islamic periods.[2]

Culture and Landmarks

The city is home to famous shrines such as those of Bahauddin Zakariya, Shah Rukn-e-Alam, and Shams Tabrizi. It is also known for its vibrant bazaars, blue pottery, and mangoes.[3]

Economy

Multan has a growing economy based on agriculture, especially cotton and mango production. It also has textile mills, handicraft industries, and a developing services sector.[4]
